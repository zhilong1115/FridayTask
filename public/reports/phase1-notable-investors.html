1. Cathie Wood / ARK Invest

Core Philosophy & Decision Framework

  • Disruptive innovation thesis: invests exclusively in companies driving technological change across genomics, robotics, AI, energy storage, blockchain, and multi-omics
  • 5-year investment horizon; targets exponential growth curves
  • Actively managed ETFs (ARKK, ARKW, ARKG, ARKF, ARKQ, ARKX)
  • Willingness to hold through extreme drawdowns — high conviction, concentrated positions
  • Publishes daily trade notifications via email and website (full transparency)

Recent Notable Positions (2025)

  • Top holding: Tesla (TSLA) — perennial largest position across multiple funds
  • Flagship ARKK holds ~41 positions, heavily weighted toward AI/autonomous driving, genomics, fintech
  • TWR of +233% since inception vs MWR of -35% — illustrating that most capital entered after peak gains (classic performance-chasing problem)
  • AUM ~$13B as of early 2025

AI Agent Learnings

  • Transparency as signal: ARK publishes every trade daily — this is a real-time, parseable signal
  • Conviction sizing: concentrated bets on high-conviction themes; position sizing reflects thesis strength
  • Contrarian timing: often buys when sentiment is worst on her names
  • Cautionary tale: TWR vs MWR gap shows that when money flows in matters enormously — sentiment tracking is key

2. Nancy Pelosi / Congressional Trading

Core Philosophy & Decision Framework

  • Trades executed by husband Paul Pelosi; Nancy disclaims involvement
  • Pattern: large positions in mega-cap tech, particularly ahead of major legislative/regulatory events
  • Heavy use of call options (leveraged upside bets) — not just stock purchases
  • Has become a cultural phenomenon — “Pelosi Tracker” apps and ETFs exist

Recent Notable Positions (Late 2025 / Jan 2026)

Per January 23, 2026 disclosure filing: - AllianceBernstein (AB): 25,000 shares ($1M-$5M) — largest direct purchase - Strong tilt toward AI and technology leaders - Mix of direct stock purchases and call option exercises spanning Dec 2025 – Jan 2026 - Historical notable picks: NVDA, AAPL, GOOGL, MSFT, CRM, RBLX (all before significant runs)

AI Agent Learnings

  • Information asymmetry signal: Congressional members may have legislative insight; trades can front-run policy
  • Options usage: Call options indicate high conviction + leverage preference
  • Timing analysis: Compare disclosure dates vs actual trade dates — the lag (up to 45 days) creates a delayed but still useful signal
  • Sector rotation: Shifts in sector allocation can signal upcoming legislative focus areas

3. Warren Buffett / Berkshire Hathaway

Core Philosophy & Decision Framework

  • Value investing: buy wonderful companies at fair prices; hold forever
  • Circle of competence — only invest in what you understand
  • Margin of safety; focus on durable competitive advantages (“moats”)
  • Prefers companies with high ROIC, pricing power, and honest management
  • Since 2025: Greg Abel increasingly involved as Buffett’s successor

4. Michael Burry / Scion Asset Management

  • Deep value contrarian: famous for “The Big Short” — betting against consensus

  • Willing to bet against the most popular trades in the market
  • Frequently rotates entire portfolio quarter to quarter

5. Top Quant Funds

Renaissance Technologies (Medallion Fund)

  • Strategy: Pure systematic/statistical arbitrage; proprietary ML models on alternative data
  • Performance: Medallion returned ~66% gross annually (1988-2018); closed to outside investors
  • Key insight: Short holding periods, massive diversification, market-neutral
  • 2024: Led quant gains with double-digit returns
  • Fee structure: 5% management + 44% performance (Medallion) — highest in industry
  • AI agent learning: Statistical edge from non-obvious data sources; speed and diversification matter; signals decay so must constantly innovate

Two Sigma

  • Strategy: AI/ML-driven investing; massive data ingestion (satellite, social, transactional)
  • Performance: Strong 2024, led quant gains alongside RenTech
  • Key insight: Science-first culture; distributed computing for market analysis
  • Fee structure: 2/20 (standard hedge fund)
  • AI agent learning: ML on alternative data can generate alpha; data infrastructure is a competitive advantage

D.E. Shaw

  • Strategy: Hybrid quantitative + fundamental; computational finance with AI integration
  • Performance: Strong H1 2024, outperformed Citadel and Millennium in some funds
  • Key insight: Blends systematic trading with discretionary macro views
  • AI agent learning: Combining quant signals with fundamental analysis (hybrid approach) can be more robust than pure quant

Citadel / Citadel Securities

  • Strategy: Multi-strategy (Ken Griffin); Citadel Securities is world’s largest market maker
  • Key insight: Market-making provides information edge; multi-strategy diversification
  • Performance: Consistently top-tier; Wellington fund famous for Crisis Alpha
  • AI agent learning: Market microstructure knowledge (order flow, bid-ask dynamics) is a powerful edge; liquidity provision as a strategy

Common Quant Fund Themes for AI Agent

  1. Alternative data is table stakes: satellite imagery, credit card data, social sentiment, web scraping
  2. Speed matters: Shorter holding periods = less exposure to regime changes
  3. Diversification: Hundreds/thousands of small positions vs. concentrated bets
  4. Signal decay: Alpha from any signal diminishes over time; constant R&D required
  5. Risk management: Position sizing, correlation monitoring, drawdown limits are paramount
  6. Execution quality: Minimizing market impact and transaction costs is a major alpha source
