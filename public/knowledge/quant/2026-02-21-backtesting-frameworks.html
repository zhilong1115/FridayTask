Why Backtesting Frameworks Matter

A strategy's merit isn't determined by gut feeling. Backtesting is the scientific experimentation phase of quant trading â€” you simulate your strategy on historical data to observe how it would have performed under real market conditions. The framework is your "laboratory."

Wrong lab â†’ wrong conclusions. Right lab â†’ fast iteration, problem discovery, and confidence building.

Core Architecture

All backtesting frameworks do the same four things:

  1. Data Engine: Load and manage historical price data
  2. Strategy Engine: Define buy/sell logic
  3. Execution Engine: Simulate order fills, slippage, commissions
  4. Analytics Engine: Calculate performance metrics

The key distinction: Event-driven vs Vectorized.

Event-Driven vs Vectorized

Event-driven: Process bar-by-bar, simulating real trading flow. Backtrader, Zipline, and QuantConnect are all event-driven. Realistic but slower.

Vectorized: Compute all signals at once with NumPy/Pandas. Fast but prone to look-ahead bias. Vectorbt and custom Pandas scripts fall here.
