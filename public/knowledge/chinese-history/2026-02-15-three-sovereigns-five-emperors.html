In China's collective memory, civilization begins with a lineage of semi-divine sage-kings known as the Three Sovereigns and Five Emperors. The "Three Sovereigns" typically refer to Fuxi, Shennong, and the Yellow Emperor, while the "Five Emperors," according to Sima Qian's Records of the Grand Historian (c. 94 BCE), are Huangdi, Zhuanxu, Di Ku, Yao, and Shun.

The Yellow Emperor is revered as the progenitor of the Huaxia people. After the Battle of Banquan unified the two brother-tribes, they defeated Chiyou at the Battle of Zhuolu, laying the foundation for the Huaxia people.
