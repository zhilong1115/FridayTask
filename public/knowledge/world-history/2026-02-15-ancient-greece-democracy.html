Fifth-century Athens witnessed humanity's first democratic experiment. Three reformers built it over 130 years: Solon abolished debt slavery; Cleisthenes created the Council of 500 and ostracism; Ephialtes transferred power from the aristocratic Areopagus to citizen juries.

Athens used sortition (lottery) rather than elections for offices, believing elections were inherently elitist. Pericles declared: "Power is in the hands not of a minority but of the whole people."
