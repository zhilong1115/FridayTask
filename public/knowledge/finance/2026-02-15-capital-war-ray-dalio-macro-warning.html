Trade War vs Capital War

You know about trade wars (tariffs, covered on 2/8). A capital war is economic confrontation at a higher level:

DimensionTrade WarCapital War
WeaponsTariffs, quotasSanctions, asset freezes, SWIFT ban
TargetFlow of goodsFlow of capital
DamageHigher pricesFrozen wealth, cut off financing
ExampleUS-China tariffsRussia 2022 sanctions

Why Is a Capital War More Dangerous?

Tariffs make things expensive, but money still flows. A capital war freezes your money. Imagine: you have $10 billion in US Treasuries and suddenly you're told "frozen, can't sell" â€” that's what happened to Russia's central bank in 2022.

When other nations see this precedent, what do they think?

"If the US can freeze Russia's money, could it freeze mine someday?"
â€” This is the core logic of Dalio's "bearish force."

The Government's Dilemma

When foreigners won't buy Treasuries, the government has two options:

  1. Raise yields to attract buyers â†’ Makes borrowing expensive, drags growth (recall our 2/1 lesson on rates vs stocks)
  2. Print money to buy its own debt (Currency Debasement) â†’ More dollars chasing same goods = inflation (recall our 2/12 CPI lesson)

In reality, it's often both â€” this is why Dalio is simultaneously bullish on gold. Gold benefits in both scenarios: high rates mean uncertainty, money printing means fiat devaluation.
