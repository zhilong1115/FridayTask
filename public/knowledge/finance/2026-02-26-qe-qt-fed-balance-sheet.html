What is QE (Quantitative Easing)?

Think of it as the Fed flying a helicopter and dropping newly printed money into the financial system.

Technically: The Fed buys Treasuries and MBS (mortgage-backed securities), injecting newly created reserves into the banking system. Banks with more reserves lend more freely, pushing down long-term interest rates.

Three effects:
1ï¸âƒ£ Lowers long-term rates (10Y Treasury yields fall)
2ï¸âƒ£ Inflates asset prices (stocks, housing, bonds rise)
3ï¸âƒ£ Weakens the dollar (more dollars circulating)


What is QT (Quantitative Tightening)?

The reverse: The Fed lets maturing bonds roll off its balance sheet without reinvesting â€” effectively vacuuming money out of the system.

Three effects:
1ï¸âƒ£ Pushes up long-term rates (more bond supply)
2ï¸âƒ£ Compresses asset prices (less liquidity)
3ï¸âƒ£ Strengthens the dollar
