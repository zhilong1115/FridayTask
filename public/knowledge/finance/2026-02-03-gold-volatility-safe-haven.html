Traditional View: Gold as Safe Haven

Gold is called a "safe haven" because:

  • No counterparty risk (unlike stocks/bonds)
  • Limited supply (can't "print" gold)
  • Millennia of proven value storage
  • Negative correlation with equities

What Went Wrong?

As Deutsche Bank's Jim Reid noted: "The recent run up in precious metals feels to have an enormous speculative element."

The problem: When a "safe haven" becomes the hot topic at dinner tables and social media, it often signals the rally is exhausting.
