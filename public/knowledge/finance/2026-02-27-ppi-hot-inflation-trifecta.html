PPI = Factory Gate Prices

Think of inflation as a river:

  1. PPI (upstream): Prices producers charge wholesalers
  2. CPI (midstream): Prices consumers see at stores
  3. PCE (downstream): The Fed's preferred comprehensive measure

PPI is a leading indicator for consumer inflation. When factory input costs rise, they eventually pass through to retail prices. It's like rising water upstream â€” it reaches downstream sooner or later.
