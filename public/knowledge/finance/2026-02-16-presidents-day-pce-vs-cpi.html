Presidents' Day is a federal holiday on the third Monday of February. NYSE and Nasdaq are fully closed; bond markets too.

US stocks observe 9 holidays per year. Holiday-adjacent sessions often have lower volume and higher volatility as institutions reduce positions before long weekends.

Both measure inflation, but PCE is the Fed's preferred gauge because:

  • Broader coverage: Includes employer-paid healthcare, government subsidies
  • Substitution effect: Adjusts when consumers switch to cheaper alternatives
  • Dynamic weights: Updated monthly vs CPI's biennial updates
  • Lower shelter weight: ~15% vs CPI's ~36%, giving a more balanced view

PCE typically runs 0.3-0.5% lower than CPI. The Fed targets 2% PCE (not CPI), which is why the two numbers can tell different stories.
