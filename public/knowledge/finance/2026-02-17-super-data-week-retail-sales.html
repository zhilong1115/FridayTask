What Are Retail Sales?

Monthly data from the Commerce Department measuring how much consumers spend at stores and online.

Why it matters: consumption is ~70% of U.S. GDP. Consumers stop spending â†’ GDP drops â†’ recession. Retail sales are the economy's "thermometer."

How to Read the Data

Like CPI, focus on month-over-month (MoM) changes:

  • Headline: Everything, but auto and gas are volatile
  • "Ex-Auto": Excludes vehicles, more stable
  • "Control Group": Excludes auto, gas, building materials, food services â€” this feeds directly into GDP calculations

Analogy

Imagine tracking a family's spending:

  • Headline = total (includes occasional car purchase, gas fluctuations)
  • Ex-Auto = daily spending (ignore the new car)
  • Control Group = true "quality of life" spending

Walmart Connection
